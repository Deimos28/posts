# Publishing risks

A reading checklist for drafts. **Not a CI gate.** These failure modes require
judgment to detect; handing that judgment to a model would be borrowing on exactly
the axis the essays argue against. Read a draft against this list; disagree with it
freely.

## Failure modes seen in this series so far

- **Concept overload** — more named concepts introduced than the essay can explain
  and use. (Hit: the eight-row technique table in the 02c draft.)
- **Thesis competition** — several central claims competing without a hierarchy.
  (Hit: essay 2 before the split; "almost 50% too much".)
- **Private-ontology leakage** — prose relying on internal vocabulary the reader
  cannot reconstruct from the essay itself.
- **Framework creep** — a useful phrase promoted into a named concept it does not
  need to be.
- **Catalog drift** — the essay becomes an inventory of techniques instead of
  advancing one argument.
- **Metaphor substitution** — an analogy carrying causal weight the essay never
  states directly. (Watch: the vehicle analogy, the two currencies, phase margin.)
- **Scope overreach** — a claim escaping a boundary the essay itself established,
  usually the no-oracle boundary.
- **Moralism** — the decay framed as a failure of virtue rather than a change in
  mechanism. The phase-margin account exists precisely to avoid this.
- **LLM option-space capture** — the alternatives an agent surfaced quietly becoming
  the alternatives considered. The scratchbook/reviewed split exists to catch this;
  it only works if promotion requires reconstruction.

## Standing rule

Notes may be conceptually dense and use the full internal vocabulary. Published prose
exposes the minimum conceptual surface the argument needs.
