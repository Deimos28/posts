## Diagram Explanation

This diagram illustrates the flow of comprehension, judgement, and coding processes.

- **Comprehension**: The starting point, involving a rated mental model.
- **Judgement**: Linked to comprehension, leading to decisions.
- **Friction**: Connected to comprehension via γ, representing challenges or resistance.
- **Manual Coding**: Receives errors from friction and sends code to the agent.
- **Agent**: Interacts with manual coding and the decision point (Code or prompt?).
- **Code or Prompt?**: Central decision point, receiving intent from manual coding and the agent, and test from judgement.

## Key Fixes
- The intent wire from "Code or prompt?" to "Manual coding" is now a straight vertical line (previously had a diagonal dog-leg).
- All connections are properly aligned with their respective boxes.
